## Theme

How to keep God's fire burning in your life without burning out, drawn from the life of Moses.

## Main Scriptures

Exodus 3 (Moses and the burning bush); Psalm 23:2-3 ("He makes me lie down... he restores my soul"); 1 Kings 19 (Elijah's burnout, where God gives him rest and food before anything else).

## Summary

Moses tried to carry God's mission in his own strength and burned out. Then he met God at the burning bush, a fire that burned without being consumed because it was sustained by God's presence, not by Moses' effort. The message gives six practical, biblical ways to avoid or recover from burnout. The heart of it: burnout happens when the fire depends on you, but a burning bush life draws its fire from God. The answer isn't better scheduling, it's a fresh encounter with God's presence.

## Key Points

1. **Manage the expectation gap.** [t=3298] Burnout often grows from the gap between what we hope for and what we actually get. Moses expected acceptance from his people but met rejection. Other people's expectations become silent demands. The freeing truth: you only have to meet the expectation of one, God himself. It's easy to please God and impossible to please everyone. Don't confuse your calling with controlling what others think.

2. **Sharpen the axe.** [t=3593] Moses' 40 years in the wilderness weren't wasted, they were a sharpening season. As Lincoln said, "Give me six hours to chop down a tree and I'll spend the first four sharpening the axe." Practically: refine your skills, improve your systems, and refresh your spirit through prayer between tasks. A few minutes of prayer doesn't cost you time, it multiplies your impact.

3. **Rest, eat, and sleep.** [t=3909] For someone who has been running hard, the most spiritual thing can simply be to rest. When Elijah was burned out, God's first gift wasn't another sermon, it was a nap and a meal. The Good Shepherd "makes me lie down," even when we're too busy to choose it ourselves.

4. **Reflect with the Word.** [t=4060] Burnout creeps in when we keep moving without stopping to hear God. But true reflection isn't replaying your pain, that only leaves you more bitter. Use Scripture as a mirror. A practical exercise: on the left of a page write your worries, grief, and disappointments; on the right, answer each with a verse of truth. And ask yourself: am I becoming stronger in God, or harder toward people? The same boiling water softens a potato but hardens an egg. What's inside determines the outcome.

5. **Restore your purpose.** [t=4502] Moses hadn't lost his calling, he'd lost his courage and confidence. Michael Jordan didn't lose his skill when he stepped away from basketball, he lost his hunger, and that's what he needed back, not more fame. God wants to renew your hunger to pursue the very thing he's already called you to.

6. **Encounter Him.** [t=4737] Before God gave Moses an assignment, he gave him an encounter. This is the ultimate answer to burnout. Better boundaries and systems help, but they aren't the source. The fire kept burning because it was sustained by God's presence. (Illustrated with two testimonies from a conference in Singapore: a woman with a heart condition healed as she ran in obedience, and an elderly woman whose unexpected fall led doctors to discover, and then see healed, a dangerous brain bleed she hadn't known about.)

## Takeaways to Reflect On

Where is an expectation gap, from others or yourself, draining you? Try fixing your eyes on God's expectation alone. Build small pockets of prayer between tasks rather than running flat out. If you've been running on empty, give yourself permission to truly rest. Try the two-column reflection exercise with Scripture. And ask where God might want to restore your hunger for something he's already placed in your hands.
