## Theme
True worship of God extends beyond singing into every area of life, expressed through clean hands, a pure heart, and humble honour toward God and others in every season.

## Main Scriptures
- Psalm 24
- Romans 11:36
- 1 Corinthians 3:1-9
- 1 Samuel 24
- 2 Samuel 6
- Colossians 1:19-22
- 1 Timothy 2:1-4
- John 4:24

## Summary
Psalm 24 opens by declaring that the earth and everything in it belongs to God, which means our lives are not our own and are meant to be lived as worship unto him. The psalm asks who may stand in God's holy place, and the answer, "the one with clean hands and a pure heart," shows that worship encompasses every area of life, including how we act toward leaders and enemies. Drawing on the life of David, Racquel illustrated what this looks like in practice, particularly David's decision to honour Saul despite having every human reason to harm him, and David's extravagant, undignified dancing as the ark returned to Jerusalem. The sermon applied these lessons to the church's current season of leadership transition, urging unity, prayer for leaders, and unashamed worship. It closed with an invitation to let the King of Glory into our lives through faith in Jesus, whose sacrifice makes it possible for us to stand before God holy and blameless.

## Key Points

1. **We are not our own, and our lives exist for God's glory** [t=2931]
   Psalm 24:1-2 declares that the earth and all who live in it belong to God. Culture tells us we are self-made and in control, but events like illness, job loss, or death remind us how little control we actually have. Romans 11:36 says all things are from him, through him, and for him, meaning our whole life is an instrument of worship to God.

2. **Worship is measured by clean hands and a pure heart in every area of life** [t=3107]
   Psalm 24:3-4 asks who may stand in God's holy place, and answers "the one with clean hands and a pure heart." Clean hands represent our external actions and words that others can see, while a pure heart refers to our internal motivations and attitudes that are hidden. Worship is not limited to singing at church but includes how we honour or dishonour God in all of life.

3. **Honouring God-appointed leadership, even when it is costly** [t=3351]
   During the church's current leadership transition, there is a human temptation to be loyal to a person and a preference rather than to the one God has anointed. Paul warned the Corinthians against division over "I follow Paul" or "I follow Apollos," calling it spiritual immaturity. God builds his church, and neither the one who plants nor the one who waters is anything, but only God who makes things grow.

4. **David's response to Saul: trusting God as judge rather than seeking revenge** [t=3680]
   When Saul entered a cave where David was hiding and David had the perfect opportunity to kill him, David instead cut only a corner of Saul's robe and was immediately conscience-stricken. He rebuked his men, honoured Saul as the Lord's anointed, and declared that God alone was the righteous judge and avenger. This is the model for how believers should respond to enemies: not with retaliation but by handing the matter to God, who vindicates those who act righteously.

5. **Loving and praying for enemies rather than praying against them** [t=4007]
   Jesus commanded his followers to love their enemies and pray for those who persecute them. 1 Timothy 2:1-4 urges prayers and intercessions for all people and all who are in positions of authority, so that we may lead peaceful and godly lives. Praying against enemies and harbouring division is an attitude God cannot bless, whereas a life of clean hands and a pure heart invites his blessing and vindication.

6. **David's extravagant, undignified public worship as the ark returned to Jerusalem** [t=4357]
   As the ark of the covenant was brought back to Jerusalem, David sacrificed a fattened calf every six steps and then stripped off his royal robes to dance and leap before the Lord in priestly linen garments. When Michal scorned him for it, David declared he would be willing to look even more foolish before the Lord. This shows that the more responsibility and prominence we carry, the more humble and undignified we must be willing to become in worship, playing to an audience of one rather than managing our reputation before people.

7. **The danger of irreverence and the gift of access through Jesus** [t=4525]
   When Uzzah reached out to steady the ark and touched it, he died because God had commanded it must never be touched. This is a reminder that God's presence is holy and not to be treated casually or with contempt. Yet through Jesus, God made a way for us to draw near without fear or shame. Colossians 1:19-22 says that through Christ's blood God has reconciled us and brought us into his presence, so we now stand before him holy and blameless.

## Takeaways to Reflect On

Take time alone with God to ask him to search your heart and reveal anything that needs repentance, particularly in how you have responded to those who have wronged you or how you have spoken about leaders behind their backs. Consider how you could practically bless or pray for someone you have regarded as an enemy. In this season of leadership transition at the church, commit to praying for both outgoing and incoming leaders rather than comparing styles or nursing preferences. Ask yourself honestly whether your worship, both private and public, is shaped by what others think of you or by a genuine desire to honour God alone. Finally, if you have not yet let the King of Glory into your life, the door is open through Jesus, whose sacrifice means you no longer have to stand before God in your own imperfect righteousness.
